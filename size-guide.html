How to Measure Your Foot

For the most accurate sizing, follow these steps to measure your foot:

Step 1: Prepare

  • Wear the socks you plan to wear with the sneakers
  • Stand on a flat surface
  • Have a ruler or measuring tape ready
  • Measure both feet (use the larger measurement)

Step 2: Measure Length

  • Place your foot on a piece of paper
  • Mark the longest point of your toe
  • Mark the back of your heel
  • Measure the distance between the marks

Step 3: Measure Width

  • Measure the widest part of your foot
  • This is usually across the ball of your foot
  • Note both length and width measurements
  • Compare with our size chart

How to Measure Your Wrist

  1. Use a flexible measuring tape or a piece of string
  2. Wrap around your wrist just below the wrist bone
  3. Note the measurement in inches or centimeters
  4. Add 0.5-1 inch for comfortable fit with the watch

Watch Strap Adjustments

  • Metal Bracelets: Can be adjusted by removing links
  • Leather Straps: Usually have multiple holes for adjustment
  • Rubber Straps: Often have micro-adjustments
  • NATO Straps: One size fits most wrists

Size Selection Tips

General Guidelines

  • Always measure your feet: Don't rely on your usual shoe size
  • Consider the brand: Different brands may fit differently
  • Think about usage: Will you wear thick socks? Consider going half a size up
  • Check reviews: Look for sizing feedback from other customers

Common Sizing Issues

  • Too Tight: Usually means you need to go up half a size
  • Too Loose: Consider going down half a size
  • Width Issues: Some models run narrow or wide
  • Break-in Period: New shoes may feel tight initially

Pro Tip

If you're between sizes, we recommend going up half a size for comfort, especially if you plan to wear the sneakers for extended periods.

Brand-Specific Sizing

Nike/Jordan

  • Generally true to size
  • Some models run narrow (Air Max, Air Force 1)
  • Consider going up half a size for wide feet

Adidas

  • Most models true to size
  • Ultraboost series may run small
  • Yeezy models often run small (go up half size)

Other Brands

  • New Balance: Generally true to size, good for wide feet
  • Puma: Slightly narrow, consider going up half size
  • Converse: Often run large, consider going down half size
